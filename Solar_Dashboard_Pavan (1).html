📊 What it means: How efficiently the solar plant converts available sunlight into electricity. A higher PR means less energy is lost to heat, soiling, and system inefficiencies.

🎯 Target: 75% is the benchmark. Above 80% is excellent; below 65% signals problems.

🌡️ Factors affecting PR: Panel temperature, soiling, shading, inverter losses, and wiring resistance.

✅ Good range: 75–85% for utility-scale solar plants.
PR = E_ac ÷ (G_poa × Capacity_kWp)

🔋Capacity Utilization Factor
🔋 What it means: The percentage of time the plant is generating at its full rated capacity. Reflects how productively the installed capacity is being used.

☀️ Solar context: Solar CUF is naturally limited by daylight hours (~12–16% min, 25–30% max for ground-mount). Monsoon months typically lower CUF.

📉 Low CUF causes: Cloudy days, grid curtailment, equipment downtime, seasonal variation.

🏆 Benchmark: 20–28% CUF is excellent for Indian solar plants.
CUF = E_actual ÷ (Cap_kW × 8760 hrs/yr)

☀️Total Irradiation — The Secret of Our Energy
🌞 Irradiance vs Irradiation: Irradiance (W/m²) is the instantaneous solar power hitting the panel. Irradiation (kWh/m²) is that power accumulated over time — the true energy input to our system.

🔭 Measurement: Captured by pyranometers mounted at panel tilt (POA = Plane Of Array), giving the most accurate real-world solar resource reading.

💡 Why it matters: Irradiation is the fundamental input that drives ALL energy production. Higher irradiation = more potential output. Low PR despite high irradiation signals losses in the system.

🌡️ Seasonal range: 90–150 kWh/m²/month typical across NJ/NY/PA.
H_poa = ∫G_poa dt (kWh/m²) — The Secret of Our Energy ☀

💸 Financial impact: Curtailed energy = lost revenue. Each MWh curtailed costs the project owner the PPA rate (~$40–80/MWh).

📡 Common causes: Grid congestion, high renewable penetration, low demand periods, transmission constraints.

🌙 Why CUF is always <30%: Solar panels only generate during daylight (6–8 hours effectively). CUF of 25% means ~6 equivalent full-load hours per day — perfectly normal!

🌤️Irradiation — The Secret of Our Energy ☀
🌞 Irradiance is everything: The sun's irradiance (W/m²) hitting your panels is the single most powerful factor in energy output. No irradiance = no electricity — it's the fundamental input of all solar energy.

📡 POA measurement: Plane-of-Array pyranometers tilt at the same angle as panels, capturing the exact solar resource your system receives — far more accurate than horizontal sensors.

🔗 Correlation insight: When energy (pink line) closely follows irradiance bars, the system is performing optimally. Gaps indicate soiling, shading, or inverter losses eating into potential.

⚡ Irradiance × Efficiency × Area = Power — This chart makes that equation visible!
G_poa (W/m²) measured → H_poa = ∫G dt (kWh/m²)

⚠️Curtailment & Downtime Analysis
⚠️ Curtailment (pink bars): Energy deliberately not exported to the grid — measured as the difference between available capacity and actual export during curtailment windows.

📴 Downtime % (grey line): Percentage of the 86-site fleet that was offline (zero generation during daylight). A site at 0 kWh on a sunny day = counted offline.

🔍 Distinguish them: Curtailment is grid-driven (not our fault). Downtime is equipment-driven (our O&M responsibility). Both drain revenue!
